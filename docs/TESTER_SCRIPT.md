# SkitBox Tester Script

This is the phone-call test. A non-technical tester should be able to do this
without opening a command prompt.

## What To Say

1. Open the unzipped SkitBox folder.
2. Double-click `START_SkitBox_WINDOWS.bat`.
3. When the browser opens, press `Show Me A Funny One`.
4. Confirm a generated skit appears.
5. Check that the side navigation shows red, amber, or green dots.
6. Follow the item marked `Next`.
7. Open Rooms.
8. Pick a room, move one cast member or prop, then press `Save Room Map`.
9. Press `Generate Scene In This Room` and confirm that room appears in the skit.
10. Press `Save This As Canon`.
11. Open Memory and confirm the saved incident appears.
12. Generate again in the same room and confirm the skit says `Previously In This Room`.
13. Open Sparks.
14. Press an example prompt button.
15. Confirm the chosen sparks change.
16. Type: `A saucer beams someone up behind police tape while two people hold hands.`
17. Press `Use Description`.
18. Confirm the chosen sparks mention UFO, Police Tape, and Heart.
19. Press `Generate From Description`.
20. Save a favourite.
21. Export TXT or HTML.
22. Close the app window, or double-click `STOP_SkitBox_WINDOWS.bat`.

## What To Watch For

- Time to first skit.
- Whether Python blocked them.
- Whether `Show Me A Funny One` was obvious.
- Whether the traffic lights made the next step clearer.
- Whether template choice made sense.
- Whether room maps made scenes feel more anchored.
- Whether `Save This As Canon` clearly felt different from Save Favourite.
- Whether the Memory page made room history and character memory understandable.
- Whether sparks felt clickable and useful.
- Whether the example prompt buttons taught what to type.
- Whether the description box made the next step obvious.
- Whether the generated skit clearly used the sparks.
- Whether save/export made sense.
- Whether stopping the app felt obvious.
- Whether any text looked scary or technical.
