# SkitBox Tester Script

This is the phone-call test. A non-technical tester should be able to do this
without opening a command prompt.

## What To Say

1. Open the unzipped SkitBox folder.
2. Double-click `START_SkitBox_WINDOWS.bat`.
3. When the browser opens, press `Show Me A Funny One`.
4. Confirm a generated skit appears.
5. Check that the side navigation shows red, amber, or green dots.
6. Follow the item marked `Next`.
7. Open Sparks.
8. Press an example prompt button.
9. Confirm the chosen sparks change.
10. Type: `A saucer beams someone up behind police tape while two people hold hands.`
11. Press `Use Description`.
12. Confirm the chosen sparks mention UFO, Police Tape, and Heart.
13. Press `Generate From Description`.
14. Save a favourite.
15. Export TXT or HTML.
16. Close the app window, or double-click `STOP_SkitBox_WINDOWS.bat`.

## What To Watch For

- Time to first skit.
- Whether Python blocked them.
- Whether `Show Me A Funny One` was obvious.
- Whether the traffic lights made the next step clearer.
- Whether template choice made sense.
- Whether sparks felt clickable and useful.
- Whether the example prompt buttons taught what to type.
- Whether the description box made the next step obvious.
- Whether the generated skit clearly used the sparks.
- Whether save/export made sense.
- Whether stopping the app felt obvious.
- Whether any text looked scary or technical.
