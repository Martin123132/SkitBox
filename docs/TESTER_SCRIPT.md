# SkitBox Tester Script

This is the phone-call test. A non-technical tester should be able to do this
without opening a command prompt.

## What To Say

1. Open the unzipped SkitBox folder.
2. Double-click `START_SkitBox_WINDOWS.bat`.
3. When the browser opens, press `Start Tester Run`.
4. Press `Generate Demo Scene` and confirm a generated skit appears.
5. Press `Save Current Scene As Canon`.
6. Press `Open Memory` and confirm the saved incident appears.
7. Return to Tester.
8. Press `Generate Remembered Scene` and confirm memory is mentioned.
9. Press `Export Share Card`.
10. Press `Copy Feedback Summary`.
11. Open Templates and export the current World Pack.
12. Open Generate and try `Copy Share Text`.
13. Open Rooms.
14. Pick a room, move one cast member or prop, then press `Save Room Map`.
15. Press `Generate Scene In This Room` and confirm that room appears in the skit.
16. Open Sparks.
17. Press an example prompt button.
18. Confirm the chosen sparks change.
19. Type: `A saucer beams someone up behind police tape while two people hold hands.`
20. Press `Use Description`.
21. Confirm the chosen sparks mention UFO, Police Tape, and Heart.
22. Press `Generate From Description`.
23. Save a favourite.
24. Export TXT and HTML.
25. Close the app window, or double-click `STOP_SkitBox_WINDOWS.bat`.

## What To Watch For

- Time to first skit.
- Whether Python blocked them.
- Whether `Show Me A Funny One` was obvious.
- Whether `Start Tester Run` was obvious.
- Whether the Tester page made the first-run path clear.
- Whether the traffic lights made the next step clearer.
- Whether template choice made sense.
- Whether World Pack export felt understandable.
- Whether room maps made scenes feel more anchored.
- Whether `Save This As Canon` clearly felt different from Save Favourite.
- Whether the Memory page made room history and character memory understandable.
- Whether sparks felt clickable and useful.
- Whether the example prompt buttons taught what to type.
- Whether the description box made the next step obvious.
- Whether the generated skit clearly used the sparks.
- Whether save, share-text copy, and share-card export made sense.
- Whether stopping the app felt obvious.
- Whether any text looked scary or technical.
